# Product Page Research Notes

Generated: 2026-05-23

## Findings Applied

- Strong pages sell a specific painful moment, not a broad category.
- Buyers need to see exact included files before they trust a downloadable pack.
- Template products work better when the page explains what the buyer can do in the first sitting.
- Bundled developer products should show one-time payment, instant-download framing, and clear package contents.
- AI template pages should name the mode of use: worksheet, checklist, clean-room example, runbook, or handoff template.
- Product pages should clearly state what is not included to avoid custom-service expectations.

## References Checked

- Hugging Face MCP Course: MCP education pages are module-oriented and promise a path from concepts to building.
- Notion AgentOS template listing: marketplace template pages make included pages and use modes explicit.
- Gumloop templates: template marketplaces emphasize reusable workflow templates and creator trust.
- DevPlaybook deals page: developer bundle pages emphasize one-time payment, instant download, and product inventory.
- Reddit digital-product discussions: repeated pattern is that specific painful moments and concrete files convert better than generic prompt/template claims.

## Page Pattern To Use

1. Hero: name the painful operational moment and what the pack helps the buyer do.
2. Proof of contents: exact ZIP artifact and actual file list.
3. First-session outcome: what the buyer can fill out or decide in 30-60 minutes.
4. Preview: short excerpt or field list from included templates.
5. Fit and non-fit: who should buy, who should not.
6. CTA: checkout plus support contact.
