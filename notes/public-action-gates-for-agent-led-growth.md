<section class="command-section notes-panel">
  <h2>Growth surfaces are not equal.</h2>
  <p>Owned-site content, Product Hunt launches, Reddit replies, direct messages, email, and paid ads all look like "marketing" from a distance. Operationally, they are different surfaces with different rules, risks, and trust costs.</p>
  <p>An owned-site note can be low-risk when it is honest, public-safe, and does not change the offer. A third-party community reply needs visible surface rules, relevance, disclosure, and restraint. Paid ads need spend caps, conversion proof, account safety, and rollback. Support-bearing messages need customer context and policy boundaries.</p>
</section>

<section class="command-section notes-panel">
  <h2>The gate should match the surface.</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Owned site:</strong> publish after copy approval and site validation.</li>
    <li><strong>Community reply:</strong> draft first, then require surface-specific rule review and manual posting.</li>
    <li><strong>Product Hunt:</strong> prepare positioning, but keep launch/account action human-led.</li>
    <li><strong>Paid ads:</strong> require spend cap, tracking proof, and platform preflight before mutation.</li>
    <li><strong>Support:</strong> draft only unless the support entitlement and customer context are verified.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="command-section notes-panel">
  <h2>Agent-led growth should leave a trail.</h2>
  <p>Every external action should record the surface, rule evidence, offer referenced, expected business outcome, rollback or follow-up, and proof that the action happened. Without that trail, the business cannot tell the difference between disciplined growth and platform spam.</p>
