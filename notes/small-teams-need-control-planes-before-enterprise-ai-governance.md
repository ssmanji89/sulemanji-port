<section class="command-section notes-panel">
  <h2>The market is moving toward control surfaces.</h2>
  <p>Large vendors are converging on a clear pattern: agent work needs governance, observability, runtime boundaries, and a way to understand where AI systems are acting across workflows. That makes sense for enterprises with many tools, many teams, and many risk owners.</p>
  <p>Small teams still have the same shape of problem. They may not need an enterprise control tower, but they do need a control plane: a place to see what the agent is allowed to do, what proof exists, what was published, what remains blocked, and what needs human judgment.</p>
</section>

<section class="command-section notes-panel">
  <h2>The lightweight version can live in GitHub.</h2>
  <p>For technical teams already running work through GitHub, the first control plane can be practical: issues, project fields, templates, release artifacts, public-safety labels, proof comments, review pages, and handoff packets.</p>
  <p>This does not replace security tooling, legal review, customer support process, or enterprise governance. It gives the team a concrete operating layer before agent work becomes too fast and too distributed to inspect.</p>
</section>

<section class="command-section notes-panel">
  <h2>Governance starts with repeatability.</h2>
  <p>A small-team control plane should answer the same questions every time: what is the intent, who has authority, what context matters, what boundary applies, what proof exists, what decision is next, and what should the next run remember?</p>
